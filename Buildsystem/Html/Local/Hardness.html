"Good" SAT representations

  • This page gives an overview of investigations into "good" SAT representations:
  • Relevant modules within the OKlibrary:
    • In module Experimentation/Investigations/BooleanFunctions we document our investigations into representations of boolean functions.
    • Good representations for cryptographic ciphers:
      • In module Experimentation/Investigations/Cryptography we document our investigations into translations of cryptographic ciphers such as AES and DES.
      • In module ComputerAlgebra/Cryptology we provide functions at Maxima-level related to cryptographic ciphers.
    • In module Satisfiability/Reductions we provide tools for computing reductions such as (generalised-)unit-clause-propagation.
    • There are also various C++ implementations of tools and generators.

Hardness and boolean function representations

  • The notion of hardness for unsatisfiable clause-sets was originally introduced in Investigating a general hierarchy of polynomially decidable classes of CNF's based on short tree-like resolution proofs and generalised in Upper and lower bounds on the complexity of generalized resolution and generalized constraint satisfaction problems .
  • See slides for a talk OK gave in the Computer Science Seminar (Leicester) May 2012 on initiating a theory of "good SAT representations", as a kind of "knowledge representation for SAT solvers" with the notion of hardness as a theoretical tool.
  • The associated technical report is Generalising unit-refutation completeness and SLUR via nested input resolution .

Good representations in cryptography

  • The Advanced Encryption Standard and Data Encryption Standard are popular encryption ciphers.
  • We try to find good translations of AES and DES by translating the components of the ciphers using representations with low hardness (see above).
  • Initial investigations were conducted in Towards a better understanding of hardness (CP 2011 Doctoral Program) and Towards a better understanding of SAT translations (LCC 2011) into the effect of using "soft" (low hardness) representations for components of the AES and DES ciphers on the performance of SAT solvers on cryptographic key discovery problems.
  • Several instances for the SAT 2011 competition .
